Instructions

  1. Scald the milk in the microwave
  2. Add sugar, salt, and 1/4 cup butter
  3. Cool to lukewarm
  4. Measure water into a large bowl
  5. Mix in yeast and wait to see if it becomes frothy, if it doesn't the yeast did not activate and you should start over
  6. Add lukewarm milk mixture
  7. In a separate bowl beat egg yolk and 1 egg white, save the remaining egg white for later
  8. Add eggs and lemon rind into yeast mixture
  9. Add 1 cup of flour and mix well
  10. Combine cornstarch and remaining flour, then stir into the yeast mixture until just mixed
  11. Refrigerate
  12. Mold 1 1/2 cups of butter into a 10x12 inch rectangle on wax paper or plastic wrap
  13. Put in the freezer for 1 hour
  14. Roll the chilled dough into a 12x16 inch rectangle
  15. place butter slab on 2/3 of the dough
  16. Fold the un-buttered section over the middle, then fold the remaining third of the dough over that
  17. Turn the dough 90 degrees and roll into a 12x16 rectangle
  18. Fold the thirds again
  19. Roll and fold one more time
  20. Refrigerate 1 hour
  21. Repeat the above 4 steps 2 more times
  22. Refrigerate overnight
  23. Shape half the dough at a time, leave the other half in the fridge
  24. Roll the dough into a 15x6 inch rectangle
  25. Cut strips to be 15x1/2 inch
  26. Twist the strips and form into spirals
  27. Place on a greased baking sheet 2 inches apart
  28. Do the same to the other half of the dough
  29. Refrigerate 1 hour
  30. Preheat oven to 375
  31. Brush the dough with the extra egg white
  32. IF SERVING IMMIDIATELY
  33. Bake until golden brown - 10-15 minutes
  34. Make toppings (Powdered sugar icing, jams or curds, creamcheese filling)
  35. Add toppings to danish and serve
  36. IF MAKING EARLY TO SERVE LATER
  37. Bake for about ten minutes, just before they are fully done
  38. Put nearly cooked danish in the freezer to store (don't store very long)
  39. When ready to serve, put the Danish in a 375 degree oven until they are fully done
  40. Make and add desired toppings
  41. Serve!
